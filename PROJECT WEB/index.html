Hyundai Motor Company, founded in 1967 and headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, is one of the world’s largest automobile manufacturers. The company is part of the Hyundai Motor Group, which also includes Kia Motors and Genesis. Hyundai has earned a global reputation for producing reliable, affordable vehicles with innovative designs and advanced technologies. Over the years, it has expanded its offerings to include a wide range of cars, from compact sedans to SUVs and hybrids. Hyundai is also known for its commitment to sustainability, focusing on developing electric vehicles (EVs) and fuel-efficient models. The company has made significant strides in autonomous driving technologies, safety features, and smart connectivity. Hyundai's vehicles are sold in over 200 countries, and the company continues to invest heavily in research and development to stay at the forefront of the global automotive industry.

  • BYD
    • BYD (Build Your Dreams) is a leading Chinese multinational company specializing in electric vehicles (EVs), batteries, and renewable energy solutions. Founded in 1995, BYD initially focused on rechargeable battery production but quickly expanded its operations into the automotive industry in 2003. Headquartered in Shenzhen, BYD has grown into one of the world’s largest EV manufacturers, offering a wide range of electric cars, buses, trucks, and rail systems. Its vehicles are celebrated for their advanced battery technology, high efficiency, and commitment to sustainability. BYD is also a pioneer in energy storage and solar power systems, making significant contributions to green energy adoption globally. The company operates in more than 50 countries, emphasizing its vision of creating a zero-emission future. Renowned for its innovation, BYD continues to lead the transition toward cleaner transportation and energy solutions.
